## Design Context

### Users

Small business owners in Colombia — barbershops, salons, clinics, spas, tutors — who need to manage client appointments. They use the dashboard from a desk and from their phone, often in the middle of a busy workday. They care about speed and clarity: they want to get things done, not admire the interface.

### Brand Personality

**Minimal, fast, practical.**

Like Vercel and Linear: functional clarity over decoration. No ornament without purpose. Every pixel earns its place. The interface should feel like it's already out of the way when you need it, and right there when you do.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **References**: Vercel (precise, sparse, confident), Linear (tight information hierarchy, every detail deliberate)
- **Anti-references**: Generic dashboard SaaS with gradients, glass cards, and icon-per-feature grids
- **Theme**: Both light and dark, equally polished. Light mode is clean and sharp; dark mode is deep, not glowing.
- **Color**: Green primary (`oklch(0.74 0.19 148)`). Neutrals should be tinted subtly toward green. No cyan-on-dark, no purple gradients.
- **Typography**: Geist Variable. Tight tracking on headings. Generous line-height on body text. Clear hierarchy — fewer sizes with more contrast between them.
- **Motion**: Minimal. Fast transitions. Exponential ease-out. No bounce.

### Design Principles

1. **Ruthless clarity** — If an element doesn't communicate something useful, remove it.
2. **Hierarchy through space** — Use generous whitespace to signal importance, tight grouping to signal relationship.
3. **Consistent rhythm** — The spacing scale must be consistent; variety is intentional, not arbitrary.
4. **Both modes, equal quality** — Light and dark are both primary. Design for both simultaneously.
5. **Mobile is a real context** — Business owners check appointments on their phones between clients. Mobile layouts must be equally functional, not just shrunk.
